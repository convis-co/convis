THE TAPE

The Tape is a short 3D horror animation inspired by the psychological tension and narrative tone of Alan Wake.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

The Tape is a short 3D horror animation inspired by the psychological tension and narrative tone of Alan Wake. The project was created as part of a Halloween series exploring cinematic storytelling through atmosphere, sound design, and first-person immersion. It blends subtle environmental detail with narrative voice-over and timed jump scares to create a compact yet impactful horror experience.

STORYBOARD

PROCESS

The piece was developed from an original 30-second script written in the style of Alan Wake’s introspective narration. The goal was to evoke unease through pacing, lighting, and sound rather than overt visual shock. Each scene was carefully designed in a first-person perspective to pull the viewer directly into the story. Using a mix of still frames, animated elements, and controlled motion, the project balanced realism with stylized tension. The eerie score was composed and later remixed into a darker trap variation for the behind-the-scenes feature, emphasizing the connection between cinematic horror and modern music production.

BEHIND THE SCENES

STILL FRAMES

OUTCOME

The final animation captures a haunting, cinematic experience where narrative, sound, and design converge. The result is a short but immersive piece that feels part film, part nightmare — showcasing how atmosphere, composition, and pacing can drive emotion even within a minimal runtime. Trick or Treat – The Tape stands as both a creative experiment in storytelling and a technical exercise in mood-driven animation.

CRITOR

Critor is a hardware wallet aimed at serious self-custody users. The brief was to create a hero animation that would introduce the device before launch.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Critor needed a launch teaser that felt more like a cinematic title-sequence than a conventional product spin. The concept was to place the wallet at the centre of a constantly shifting “data-storm”: glowing price streams, coordinate read-outs, crystalline prisms and robotic arms. The aim was to dramatise two ideas at once—unbreakable physical security and high-speed digital connectivity.

STORYBOARD

PROCESS

I mapped the story in nine seamless beats, sketching a single continuous camera path that threads through each moment using match cuts—close-ups, hand-off, chain repetition, and final hero pedestal—so the viewer never experiences a hard cut. During look-development I balanced the wallet’s matte blacks against an electric cyan-blue environment, layering procedural rubber grain, brushed-metal sheens, diffraction shards, and pulsing light trails to evoke a “data storm” swirling around the device. For the final animation each scene element—coordinate read-outs, tracking boxes, prisms, robotic arms, and liquid-shader hands—was timed to the master camera move, allowing text and light to drift naturally across the lens while keeping the wallet perfectly sharp and centre-frame. The result is a fluid animation that feels equal parts product reveal and cinematic title sequence.

STYLEFRAMES

VIEWPORT RENDER

OUTCOME

The finished animation frames Critor as a calm anchor amid motion: cascading cryptocurrency tickers sweep across the scene in a subtle “Matrix” motif, while iridescent crystal shards glint to suggest stored value. That interplay of kinetic data and tactile materials underscores the wallet’s promise—digital wealth, physically protected. Visually rich yet concise, the piece serves as a launch teaser that sparks curiosity.

HEN TITAN

For this project, I collaborated with Ask Tony Design to create a dynamic 3D animation for HEN (Hen Nozzles) that brings their firefighting tool—the Hen Titan—to life.

PROCESS

I began by studying HEN's CAD data to ensure accuracy in the 3D model. Once the base geometry was imported, I refined all critical components—the rotating base, the azimuth/elevation mechanisms, the adjustment ring with "Near" and "Far" detents, and the outlet nozzle tip—so that each part could articulate exactly as it would in real life. After finalizing the high-resolution model, I created PBR textures that balanced rugged, functional metal finishes with subtle wear marks. This gave the Titan a realistic, industrial look without feeling overly distressed.

OUTCOME

The final animation runs just under 60 seconds and effectively balances real-world context with technical clarity. By visually demonstrating the Titan's modular adjustability—both in how it mounts on a rig and how an operator can switch from pinpoint jet to full-coverage—the animation helps communicate the product's key selling points in under a minute.

I incorporated aesthetic styles such as futuristic minimalism, 3D holographic effects, and glowing block animations to enhance the visual narrative. The animation sequences were meticulously planned to synchronize with the voice-over, ensuring a cohesive flow of information and visuals.
